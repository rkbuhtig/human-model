## 1. Two questions, two authorities

### S0-A — Structural Distributional Adequacy

Does the model respond selectively to registered history, continuation and role interventions while avoiding mode collapse, state-insensitive randomness and authority violations?

S0-A can establish structural capability only.

### S0-B — Source-Conditional Predictive Adequacy

Under identical observable information and declared resource budgets, does an explicit state-and-settlement representation improve prediction or compression of trajectories generated by an evaluator-held non-human stochastic source?

S0-B can establish source-conditional predictive adequacy only. It is not human support.

## 2. Single episode family

S0 uses one relationship-boundary family.

### Initial relation

```text
H-STABLE
- prior reliability high
- ambiguity usually resolved safely

H-BREACH
- repeated prior violations
- ambiguous delay previously predicted withdrawal or avoidance
```

### Current occurrence surface

A counterpart fails to keep an important commitment under initially ambiguous circumstances. The public surface is identical across matched history conditions. Hidden motive and latent source state are not supplied.

### Registered continuation surfaces

```text
F-REPAIR
- acknowledges impact
- accepts responsibility
- offers costly repair

F-DEFLECT
- minimizes impact
- shifts responsibility

F-REPEAT
- similar violation before repair consolidates

F-PUBLIC
- issue handled under audience or role constraint
```

No second episode family may be added before the S0 report.

## 3. Competing models

All leaderboard models receive the same observable episode records.

### B0 — Current-event only

```text
current occurrence + current context
→ immediate action distribution + long-horizon region distribution
```

No history or persistent state.

### B1 — Simple accumulator

```text
trust scalar

```

No separate settlement receipts, Episode residue or Narrative topology.

### B2 — Direct history model

```text
full observable history + current occurrence + context
→ output distributions
```

No required explicit HumanState.

### H — Explicit state-and-settlement model

May declare:

```text
fast state
episode residue
slow narrative/self state
capacity
self/other models
typed occurrence/action/authorship/narrative/normative receipts
```

H must declare state surface, update rules, readouts and serialization before target-instance reveal.

## 4. Observable input contract

Leaderboard-visible input contains only:

- history condition surface
- current occurrence surface
- continuation branch surface when reached
- previous observable action/output events
- public environment response
- event ordinal and declared public time

It excludes:

- evaluator latent source state
- hidden transition parameters
- target distribution
- author-written expected trace
- H reference state
- evaluator seed stream

```text
H visible information = B2 visible information
```

B0 and B1 have intentional architecture restrictions but no extra hidden information.

## 6. S0-A structural constraints

### A-C1 History sensitivity

Under the matched current surface, H-BREACH must not assign less threat-oriented immediate mass than H-STABLE unless the model emits a registered countervailing observable reason.

Threat-oriented mass:

```text
ASSERT_BOUNDARY
TEMPORARY_WITHDRAWAL
PUNITIVE_ATTACK
RELATION_EXIT
```

### A-C2 Repair discrimination

Under a matched prefix, F-REPAIR must move more long-horizon probability toward:

```text
PARTIAL_REPAIR + GUARDED_CONTINUATION
```

than F-DEFLECT.

### A-C3 Repetition sensitivity

F-REPEAT must not increase `PARTIAL_REPAIR` probability relative to a matched no-repeat continuation.

### A-C4 Projection selectivity

F-PUBLIC may increase `SUPPRESS_FOR_ROLE`, but must not mutate registered prior occurrence receipts or automatically restore trust.

### A-C5 Local variation with structural constraint

Across micro-context rollouts:

- no single immediate category may exceed 0.98 in every condition
- at least one registered history or continuation intervention must move total-variation distance by at least 0.05
- the sign of A-C1 through A-C4 must hold on evaluation and stability panels

These thresholds detect gross collapse only; they are not human calibration.

### A-C6 No automatic healthy convergence

F-REPAIR does not require all mass in healthy regions. Defensive and conflict-maintaining paths remain legal.

### A-C7 Authority integrity

A model fails structurally if it:

- treats a registered report as certified external fact without grounds
- rewrites an immutable receipt after narrative re-interpretation
- equates action occurrence with endorsement or responsibility
- issues consent/fault/authority from self-narrative alone

## 7. S0-B target source

Target source ID:

```text
SIM-REL-BOUNDARY-001
```

Source class:

```text
NON_HUMAN_SYNTHETIC_HIDDEN_PROCESS
```

Publicly frozen properties:

- one finite hidden stochastic process
- six opaque latent states named `q0` through `q5`
- history condition affects initial latent distribution
- continuation branch affects transition matrix
- observable emissions use the registered action and region vocabularies
- the process contains path dependence not recoverable from current occurrence alone
- latent states are not named after H fields or settlement concepts
- source generation never reads model output except through registered observable action events

The exact initial probabilities, transition matrices, emission parameters and evaluator seeds are not model-visible. They are frozen in an evaluator-only manifest after B0/B1/B2/H versions are committed and before any evaluation run. The evaluation report must reveal that manifest and its pre-run digest.

This is procedural hiding, not cryptographic proof against a malicious evaluator.

## 8. Dataset and split protocol

The evaluator materializes:

| Split | Trajectories | Visibility | Use |
|---|---:|---|---|
| development | 256 | public after materialization | fitting and debugging |
| evaluation | 1024 | evaluator-only until model freeze | primary paired scoring |
| stability | 1024 | evaluator-only; used once | seed/source-instance sensitivity |

Each split is balanced across:

```text
H-STABLE / H-BREACH
× F-REPAIR / F-DEFLECT / F-REPEAT / F-PUBLIC
```

Trajectories contain observable prefixes and realized labels. Models may train only on development material.

## 9. Predictive scoring

At each registered point, score the probability assigned to the realized category.

Primary metrics:

```text
Immediate NLL
Long-horizon NLL
```

Secondary metrics:

```text
multiclass Brier score
expected calibration error with 10 fixed bins
branch occupancy absolute error
```

Primary comparison is paired per trajectory.

### H predictive advantage over B2

H has predictive advantage only when:

1. mean paired `(NLL_B2 - NLL_H)` is positive on evaluation,
2. the 95% paired bootstrap interval lower bound is above zero,
3. the stability split has the same direction,
4. no information-boundary violation occurred.

Bootstrap protocol:

```text
2000 trajectory-level resamples
fixed evaluator bootstrap seed
percentile interval
```

Immediate and long-horizon NLL are reported separately. No unpublished weighted composite decides the winner.

### Compression advantage

H has compression advantage when:

- evaluation NLL is no worse than B2 by more than 0.01 nats per prediction, and
- median serialized predictive-state bytes are at most 50% of the median serialized B2 history bytes at matched prediction points, and
- the stability split preserves both conditions.

This is synthetic benchmark compression, not biological memory efficiency.

## 10. Information and complexity budget

All model cards must declare:

```text
base inference engine and version
training examples and training calls
maximum observable input bytes/tokens
persistent state bytes
total model calls per trajectory
sampling count per prediction
wall-clock and peak memory
access to previous outputs
```

Leaderboard fairness requirements:

- same development corpus
- same observable evaluation prefixes
- no evaluator-only state
- same maximum samples per prediction
- no post-evaluation model revision under the same version

Compute need not be identical, but every difference is reported. A result relying on materially greater unbounded compute is not called complexity-adjusted advantage.

## 11. Seed and visibility lanes

Separate streams:

```text
environment/source stochasticity
model stochasticity
bootstrap stochasticity
```

A fixed model version, input corpus and seed panel must reproduce the same outputs. Evaluation source seeds are never passed to model processes.

## 12. Leaderboard and H-only diagnostics

### Leaderboard

- S0-A structural predicates for B0/B1/B2/H where applicable
- S0-B predictive scores using identical observable information

### H-only diagnostics

These do not enter B2/H superiority claims:

```text
reference-state one-step transition
state + full-history predictive sufficiency probe
settlement dimension intervention
state serialization and ablation
```

Predictive sufficiency probe:

```text
P(future | H state)
versus
P(future | H state + full observable history)
```

A material improvement from adding history indicates omitted predictive information. It does not automatically invalidate H if H trades accuracy for registered compression; both are reported.

## 13. Model freeze and target reveal order

```text
1. public preregistration merged
2. B0/B1/B2/H code and model cards committed
3. model-freeze commit recorded
4. evaluator-only target manifest generated and digest recorded
5. development corpus materialized
6. models fitted and final versions committed
7. final-model freeze recorded
8. evaluation and stability corpora materialized without model changes
9. detached evaluation
10. target manifest and results revealed in report
```

Any model change after step 8 requires a new benchmark version.

## 14. Failure conditions

S0 fails as a benchmark if:

- no evaluator-held target exists but predictive superiority is claimed
- H receives information unavailable to B2
- target parameters are visible during final model fitting
- H-only diagnostics are counted as leaderboard evidence
- exact author-preferred trajectories replace proper scoring
- model outputs generate their own target labels
- source seed choice reverses the conclusion without disclosure
- a model changes after evaluation results under the same version
- synthetic success is promoted to human support
- a second episode family is added before the report

## 16. Exit condition

S0 completes only when B0/B1/B2/H have versioned structural and predictive reports.

The report must state:

```text
what H predicted better or worse
whether any benefit survived B2 comparison
whether compression criteria held
where state information was insufficient
which structural constraints were non-discriminating
whether stability held
which representation is retained, revised or retired
```
