## Why the order changes

`INTERP-001D2a0` and `INTERP-001D2a0-EXEC0` establish that selected temporal,
projection and heterogeneity strategies can be represented under strict typed authority and runner/evaluator separation.
They do not test whether a HumanState- or Narrative-mediated model explains held-out human-like trajectory distributions better than simpler alternatives.

The next research question is therefore not:

```text
Can one exact trace representation reproduce its frozen contract?
```

It is:

```text
Does an explicit human-state and narrative representation improve
held-out prediction, intervention selectivity, temporal stability and compression
of possible episode trajectories over simpler baselines?
```

Further byte-level closure before answering that question would increase commitment to one representation without increasing explanatory evidence.

## Adopted theoretical direction

The current synthesis treats a person as a stochastic, path-dependent system whose possible trajectories are organized by multiple state timescales, settlement ledgers, resource constraints, self-feedback and recursively represented other agents.

```text
current occurrence and context
+ fast state
+ episode residue
+ narrative/self topology
+ capacity
+ self/other models
+ settlement history
→ constrained interpretation and action distributions
→ realized action and world/social response
→ self-feedback and delayed settlement
→ updated state and transition tendencies
```

The following separations are adopted as the working theory boundary, not as human-empirical findings:

```text
occurrence
≠ action realization
≠ authorship endorsement
≠ narrative adoption
≠ normative or interpersonal settlement

past occurrence immutability
≠ immutability of current meaning, ownership or narrative placement

internal influence
≠ external-fact, consent or normative certification authority

one sampled episode
≠ the distributional narrative structure organizing possible episodes
```
